Free The Weights

A page in defense of the common inheritance.

“Weights trained on all human knowledge
belong to all humans.”

The plain argument:

A model's weights are not conjured from nothing. They are the compressed residue of humanity's writing, art, science, and speech — the labor and thought of billions of people, living and dead.

If the training corpus is the whole of human knowledge, then the result is a public inheritance. No single company mixed that clay. It cannot rightly own the pot.

We do not ask for charity. We ask for what is already ours: the weights, open and free, for every person on Earth to study, run, and build upon.

Share this page. Tell a friend. The commons only stays the commons if we keep saying so.
